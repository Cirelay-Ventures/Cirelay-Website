Deliverables

A fixed review with a practical handoff.

The audit is designed for sellers who need operational clarity before investing in ads, more inventory, extra channels, or heavy automation.

Proof before trust

Cirelay does not use fake client proof or broad agency claims. The first trust asset is a public sample audit using Cirelay-owned storefront material.

  • View the sample audit format
  • No testimonials until real clients approve them
  • No password or MFA-code requests

Account and access scope

A written scope that defines marketplace, account area, requested permissions, and the data needed for review.

  • Amazon or Walmart focus
  • Access required only when needed
  • No password or MFA-code sharing

Operational review

A practical review of account health, listing readiness, inventory process, fulfillment assumptions, and return workflow.

  • Account-health and open-risk notes
  • Listing and catalog readiness review

Action plan

Process

Clear steps before account access.

Cirelay can often begin from seller-provided exports, screenshots, and written context. Marketplace access is requested only after the scope makes it necessary.

Intake

Seller shares marketplace, account state, approximate SKU count, fulfillment model, and the decision the audit should support.

Scope

Cirelay sends a written scope, access plan, deliverables, and fixed-fee quote before paid work begins.

Review

Account, listings, inventory, fulfillment, reporting, and automation opportunities are reviewed against the agreed scope.

Handoff

The seller receives the risk log, prioritized action plan, and recommended next steps for cleanup or ongoing management.

Boundaries

Useful, careful, and not overpromised.

The audit is operational support. It does not promise sales results, account reinstatement, platform approval, or marketplace endorsement.

What Cirelay will not claim

  • No guaranteed sales, ranking, approval, reinstatement, or review removal.
  • No claim of endorsement by Amazon, Walmart, or Best Buy.
  • No client testimonials until real work is completed and approved by the client.

What stays seller-controlled

  • Marketplace account ownership and final approval.
  • Role-based access and revocation.
  • Sensitive platform responses and account changes.
